Approach

Convoluted Neural Network allows us to view an image through multiple layers formed with millions of neurons. We gain more information of an image from analyzing neurons in different layers than pixels directly. In order to construct a styled photo, we need to define two forms of loss function, namely content loss and style loss. The former captures how similar the resulted image is in terms of the actual content to the original photo whereas the latter characterizes the artistic strokes and patterns that are defined by the styled painting.

Model and Parameters

We used VGG-Network, a Convolutional Neural Network that rivals human performance on a common visual object recognition benchmark task. There are 16 convolutional and 5 pooling layers. The model is publicly available and can be explored in the caffe-framework. All the feature maps and parameters are directly applied from this model to our project. We then ran through combinations of weights on the content loss and style loss functions, and examined which combination yields the most visually appealing results.
